About TLUG Technical Meetings

TLUG technical meetings are open to all Linux users and friends. Whether you're a seasoned system administrator, a developer, or just getting started with Linux, you're welcome to join us. Our presentations cover a wide range of topics from system administration and development to new technologies and tools in the Linux ecosystem.

These meetings provide an excellent opportunity to:

  • Learn about new Linux technologies and tools
  • Share knowledge and experiences with fellow Linux enthusiasts
  • Network with professionals in the Tokyo tech community
  • Get help with Linux-related questions and challenges

Presentations are typically in English, but questions and discussions can be in either English or Japanese. All skill levels are welcome!

About TLUG Nomikai

Our nomikai (drinking parties) are informal social gatherings where TLUG members and friends get together to relax, chat, and enjoy good food and drinks. These events are primarily social, though Linux and technology topics often come up naturally in conversation.

Nomikai events are perfect for:

  • Meeting other Linux users in a relaxed setting
  • Casual discussions about technology, work, and life in Tokyo
  • Building friendships within the community
  • Newcomers to get acquainted with TLUG members

Everyone is welcome, including non-Linux users who are curious about our community!

About TLUG Meetings

The Tokyo Linux Users Group (TLUG) has been bringing together Linux enthusiasts in Tokyo since 1994. Our meetings provide a forum for learning, sharing knowledge, and connecting with fellow users of Linux and open source software.

All meetings are open to the public and free to attend. We welcome users of all experience levels, from beginners to experts, and encourage participation from anyone interested in Linux, open source software, or technology in general.

About TLUG

The Tokyo Linux Users Group is one of the oldest and most active Linux user groups in Japan. Founded in 1994, we've been promoting Linux and open source software in the Tokyo area for over two decades.

  • Please be respectful of all attendees and speakers
